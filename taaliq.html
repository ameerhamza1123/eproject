Taaliq Calligraphy

Taaliq is a flowing script used for poetic and artistic purposes, known for its distinctive slanted style.

Taaliq calligraphy developed in the 14th century and is characterized by its cursive and flowing nature. It is often used in poetry and artistic writings, where its elegant and fluid strokes add a lyrical quality to the text.

The script’s unique style and artistic potential make it a popular choice for calligraphers looking to create visually striking and expressive works. Taaliq’s graceful lines and elaborate curves contribute to its aesthetic appeal and artistic versatility.

Examples of Taaliq calligraphy can be found in various poetic manuscripts and artistic works, showcasing its beauty and fluidity.
